## What moved the needle
- **Game integrity:** killed the 0-question guess-win (HUNCH, rank "?", truth sealed); a wrong
  accusation seals the truth (no spoiler/leak) — and a *worked* wrong call is a NEAR_MISS (rank D),
  not a flat F.
- **Difficulty feel:** model-judged **severity (0–3) → deterministic delta** (−2/−9/−16/−26).
  Lowering the model temperature to 0.2 collapsed the run-to-run variance that blocked Devon & Sam
  (same question was −2..−26, now a tight adjacent-tier band). Sharp/evidence-anchored hits land hard
  and consistently; filler floored at −2 so it can't grind a low case down; **repeat questions → 0**
  ("you already asked that").
- **BLUFF is first-class:** a bluff that *names the truth or is plausible & undisprovable* lands;
  an absurd one is scoffed off — discrimination works on player-written bluffs, not just authored chips.
- **In-room payoff:** the crack always delivers a real confession; every question shows a one-line
  `read` ("named the curry stain — landed") that teaches the suspect.
- **Content/replay:** 3 → **7 cases** (comedy / Singlish / forgery / dating / history / sci-fi),
  each with an AI noir portrait; spoiler-free share card with **day number + streak**.
- **UX:** room CASE FILE panel fills the old empty void; ACCUSE separated with a stakes hint;
  theory order shuffled; SFX on by default.

## Strengths the jury called out (preserve)
Writing & distinct voices ("Obra-Dinn-tier confessions"), airtight robustness (prompt-injection
resisted, no truth leak, clean 4xx states), cohesive noir presentation, real daily hook.
